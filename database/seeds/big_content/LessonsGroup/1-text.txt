<h3 style="text-align: center;"><strong>There are two tenses in English &ndash; past and present</strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong><em>The present tenses in English are used:</em></strong></p>
<ul>
<li>to talk about the&nbsp;<strong>present</strong></li>
<li>to talk about the&nbsp;<strong>future</strong></li>
<li>to talk about the&nbsp;<strong>past&nbsp;</strong>when we are telling a story in&nbsp;<strong>spoken&nbsp;</strong>English or when we are summarising a book, film, play etc.</li>
</ul>

<p><br />We use these forms:</p>
<ul>
<li>to talk about the&nbsp;<strong>present</strong>:</li>
</ul>
<p class="rteindent1" style="text-align: center;">He&nbsp;<strong>works&nbsp;</strong>at McDonald&rsquo;s. He&nbsp;<strong>has worked&nbsp;</strong>there for three months now.<br />He&nbsp;<strong>is working&nbsp;</strong>at McDonald&rsquo;s. He&nbsp;<strong>has been working&nbsp;</strong>there for three months now.<br />London&nbsp;<strong>is&nbsp;</strong>the capital of Britain.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong><em>The past tense in English is used:</em></strong></p>
<ul>
<li>to talk about the&nbsp;<strong>past</strong></li>
<li>to talk about&nbsp;<strong>hypotheses&nbsp;</strong>&ndash; things that are imagined rather than true.</li>
<li>for&nbsp;<strong>politeness</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<p>We use these forms:</p>
<ul>
<li>to talk about the&nbsp;<strong>past</strong>:</li>
</ul>
<p class="rteindent1" style="text-align: center;">He&nbsp;<strong>worked&nbsp;</strong>at McDonald&rsquo;s. He&nbsp;<strong>had worked&nbsp;</strong>there since July..<br />He&nbsp;<strong>was working&nbsp;</strong>at McDonald&rsquo;s. He&nbsp;<strong>had been working&nbsp;</strong>since July.</p>
<ul>
<li>to refer to the&nbsp;<strong>present&nbsp;</strong>or&nbsp;<strong>future&nbsp;</strong>in&nbsp;<strong>conditions</strong>:</li>
</ul>
<p class="rteindent1" style="text-align: center;">He could get a new job if he really&nbsp;<strong>tried</strong>.<br />If Jack&nbsp;<strong>was playing&nbsp;</strong>they would probably win.</p>
<p class="rteindent1">and&nbsp;<strong>hypotheses</strong>:</p>
<p class="rteindent1" style="text-align: center;">It might be dangerous. Suppose they&nbsp;<strong>got&nbsp;</strong>lost.<br />I would always help someone who really&nbsp;<strong>needed&nbsp;</strong>help.</p>
<p class="rteindent1">and&nbsp;<strong>wishes</strong>:</p>
<p class="rteindent1" style="text-align: center;">I wish it&nbsp;<strong>wasn&rsquo;t&nbsp;</strong>so cold.</p>
<ul>
<li>In&nbsp;<strong>conditions</strong>,&nbsp;<strong>hypotheses&nbsp;</strong>and&nbsp;<strong>wishes</strong>, if we want to talk about the&nbsp;<strong>past</strong>, we always&nbsp;use the&nbsp;<strong>past perfect</strong>:</li>
</ul>
<p class="rteindent1" style="text-align: center;">I would have helped him if he&nbsp;<strong>had asked</strong>.<br />It was very dangerous, What if you&nbsp;<strong>had got&nbsp;</strong>lost?<br />I wish I&nbsp;<strong>hadn&rsquo;t spent&nbsp;</strong>so much money last month.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>We can use the past forms&nbsp;to talk about the&nbsp;<strong>present&nbsp;</strong>in a few&nbsp;<strong>polite expressions</strong>:</li>
</ul>
<p class="rteindent1" style="text-align: center;">Excuse me, I&nbsp;<strong>was wondering&nbsp;</strong>if this&nbsp;<strong>was&nbsp;</strong>the train for York.<br />I just&nbsp;<strong>hoped&nbsp;</strong>you&nbsp;<strong>would&nbsp;</strong>be able to help me.</p>
